How this could be used at WeWork

In a real sales or growth team, this workflow could sit behind a lead form or CRM intake. Incoming enquiries could be classified by urgency, team size, location, and product fit; high-intent leads could receive faster human-reviewed follow-up, while lower-priority enquiries move into nurture. The goal is not to replace sales teams, but to reduce manual triage and improve speed-to-lead while keeping approval in control.

Q1 Plan

A first 90 days could create visible proof: content live, pages live, demand signal live.

Day 1 to Day 90.

Three phases. Five actions per phase. Each phase ships a measurable output, not just a planning artefact.

Days 1-30Publish the assets, launch measurement, and test whether non-branded demand can move.
Days 31-60Build city/product pages and test LinkedIn demand against enterprise buyers.
Days 61-90Validate Kochi with outreach, waitlist signal, and priced LOI conversations.

Publish Blog Post 1 (coworking cost breakdown)

Metric:indexed in Google within 7 days; early ranking movement and 500+ directional organic visits within 30 days.
Dependency:none for portfolio draft; internal review would come before publication.
Why now:a low-budget signal for whether non-branded content can move a 4,400/mo keyword WeWork appears under-represented on.

Publish Blog Post 2 (Bengaluru neighbourhood guide)

Metric:300+ directional organic visits in 30 days; 2+ internal links from existing site pages live.
Dependency:none (drafted, cross-linked to Post 1).
Why now:stacks intent against Post 1, builds the topical cluster the audit recommends, and seeds the Bengaluru city page work in Phase 2.

Launch the 5-post LinkedIn series

Metric:1 post per week for 5 weeks; modelled target of 50K+ combined impressions; 3+ enterprise conversations as an attribution stretch target.
Dependency:internal brand/legal review before any executive-voice post is used.
Why now:the executive-voice drafts test leadership tone and seed the Tier-2 narrative as a discussion asset before any internal pilot decision.

Run the non-branded keyword gap audit live with the SEO/content team

Metric:modelled backlog of 40 target keywords (city, building, product) shared with content + dev leads by Day 21; alignment meeting closed with named owners per cluster.
Dependency:1 hour each from SEO lead and web dev lead.
Why now:the audit suggests cofynd.com pulls materially more traffic from an overlapping keyword universe at lower DR; this is a high-priority hypothesis to validate with internal analytics.

Stand up weekly non-branded organic dashboard

Metric:live Tableau/Looker view tracking non-branded sessions, top-50 keyword count, and conversion to demo request — shared with the CMO by Day 30.
Dependency:GA4 + Search Console access.
Why now:every action that follows needs a measurement spine; without it Q1 wins are anecdotes.

Ship city landing pages for Bengaluru, Noida, Mumbai

Metric:all 3 pages live by Day 55; directional organic lift by Day 90; top-50/top-30 movement for at least one city "coworking space in [city]" term.
Dependency:web dev (est. 8 dev days), 1 designer week.
Why now:the audit suggests a 4,100–5,600/mo opportunity where Awfis already ranks and WeWork appears under-represented.

Rebuild the Virtual Office product page

Metric:live by Day 60; early ranking movement for "virtual office" within 60 days of publish; 20+ qualified VO leads as a stretch target.
Dependency:product marketing input on VO pricing tiers, web dev (3 days).
Why now:the audit suggests a 3,000/mo standalone opportunity where Virtual Office could map cleanly to non-branded acquisition.

Launch a paid LinkedIn acquisition test against Tier-1 enterprise decision-makers

Metric:modelled ₹2 lakh test budget; CPL under ₹3,500 as a benchmark target; 8+ booked enterprise demos as a stretch target.
Dependency:₹2L paid budget approval, HubSpot landing page setup.
Why now:tests whether the LinkedIn organic traction from Days 1–30 converts under paid amplification, and gives a CAC benchmark before the Tier-2 pilot needs one.

Internal linking overhaul across top 50 pages

Metric:200+ contextual internal links added; reduced homepage traffic concentration; 15+ secondary pages targeted for top-100 movement.
Dependency:SEO contractor or 1 week of focused time.
Why now:the audit's #7 priority but nearly free; compounds every other content asset shipped in Phase 1 and Phase 2.

Publish 2 more blog posts in the cost/comparison cluster

Metric:live by Day 60; 800+ organic visits as a modelled 30-day stretch target.
Dependency:content writer or 1 week of focused time.
Why now:Posts 1 and 2 will have proven the cluster works; doubling down is cheaper than starting a new content vertical.

Run a Kochi 90-day demand validation sprint

Metric:modelled target of 200+ qualified Infopark/Smartworld outreach contacts; 20+ priced LOI conversations; go/no-go signal using ≥10% qualified-outreach conversion as a working threshold.
Dependency:real estate + sales coordination, ₹3L outbound + landing page budget.
Why now:the validation gate happens before fit-out spend; if signal is weak, the modelled investment could be redirected to a stronger proven market.

Ship building/neighbourhood landing pages for top 10 tech parks

Metric:10 pages live by Day 90; top-50/top-30 movement for selected tech park keywords (8,100/mo opportunity from 3 keywords alone).
Dependency:web dev (12 dev days), local content research.
Why now:Awfis appears to capture building-name demand today; dedicated pages could help WeWork compete where its centres already have physical relevance.

Launch Kochi pre-launch landing page + waitlist

Metric:300+ waitlist signups as a modelled target from Infopark-targeted outreach + paid; signups feed into LOI qualification.
Dependency:web dev (2 days), ₹1L paid budget.
Why now:gives the Tier-2 demand validation a measurable digital surface and produces the priced LOI evidence the gating condition requires.

Hand off content engine to a hired/contracted writer

Metric:1 new ranking-ready post per week from Day 75 onward; in-house content time reduction tracked as an operating target.
Dependency:1 hire or contractor (₹60K–₹80K/mo).
Why now:the engine works in Phase 2; scaling it requires the operator off the keyboard and onto strategy by Day 90.

Expand city pages to Pune, Hyderabad, Chennai, Gurgaon

Metric:all 4 live by Day 90; 800+ organic visits as a modelled first-month stretch target.
Dependency:web dev (8 dev days).
Why now:template exists from Phase 2, marginal cost per page is low, and these complete the Tier-1 city footprint before Q2 attention shifts to Tier-2.

Campaign Concept

The campaign explores one claim: Kochi is developing Tier-1 ambition, and workplace standards can reflect that growth.

If WeWork launches Kochi — this is what runs.

One product, one city, one claim: the building has finally caught up to the work.

The Building Catches Up.

Kochi is building at global speed. Its workplace should keep pace.

A potential WeWork Kochi launch positioned around enterprise-grade flex for the GCCs and founders already there.

Variant 1 — The Standard, Finally Local

Audience
GCC India heads and global operations leaders evaluating Kochi for a captive build-out.
Headline
Your London board. Your Kochi floor. Same standard.
Body
A potential WeWork Kochi centre framed around enterprise-grade flexible workspace for Kerala’s GCC pipeline.
Proof point
Kerala’s GCC Playbook targets growth from 40 to 120 GCCs and 200,000 professionals by 2030. Source: Kerala GCC Playbook.
Lead format
LinkedIn document post + 1:1 BD outreach sequence to named GCC leaders.

Variant 2 — A Cleaner P&L, A Faster Move

Audience
Mid-market Indian CEOs and CFOs (100–500 employees) sizing up a Kochi expansion or HQ shift.
Headline
Move to Kochi in 30 days. Not 9 months.
Body
A modelled WeWork Private Office in Kochi at ₹15,000 a seat: fully fitted, serviced, and positioned to reduce fit-out capex and lease friction. Scale up or down by the floor.
Proof point
Modelled ₹15,000 Private Office seat / ₹13,200 blended; designed to reduce upfront fit-out capex and lease complexity. Exact accounting and terms vary by contract.
Lead format
60-second founder film + a gated TCO calculator landing page.

Variant 3 — Build the Team, Not the Lease

Audience
Series B+ founders running split teams between Bengaluru/Mumbai and Kochi.
Headline
Hire in Kochi this quarter. Defer the lease decision.
Body
A Kochi access product could test whether satellite teams and founders value national network flexibility without committing to a full private office.
Proof point
Official All Access Plus benchmark: ₹15,000 + tax/month; any Tier-2 access offer should be treated as a modelled pricing experiment. Source: WeWork India All Access Plus public pricing.
Lead format
WhatsApp creative + founder-network landing page, with potential partner/community distribution subject to access and approval.

Channel mix

35% of mix LinkedIn (organic + paid)

Highest-intent reach for GCC leaders, CFOs and Series B+ founders; carries the document post, founder film and ABM retargeting.
